Welcome to the Notes and Interpretation Course!

This is a module of the larger UCRE course and will cover everything you need to know about note taking during interviews and using notes to interpret interview resutls.

Introduction

This course will cover the following learning objectives:

Note Taking
  • When prompted, Students can state 3 reasons why note taking is important during interviews.
  • Given an interview situation, students can state at least one way to prepare before the interview to take notes effectively.
  • Given an interview situation, students can identify the most appropriate technique for note taking, in most cases.
  • Given an interview situation, students understand the responsibilities of a note taker, before, after and during the interview.
  • Given an interview situation, students understand and can choose the appropriate tools for note taking.
  • Given previously made interview notes, students can identify most common errors in the notes.

Interpretation
  • When prompted, students can state at least two reasons why note taking is important for the interpretation process.
  • When prompted, students can explain clearly what an interpretation note is and how to use it.
  • When given interview notes, students can create interpretation notes in the standardized structure.
  • When given interview notes, students can create interpretation notes in the standardized structure.
  • Given previously made interpretation notes, students can identify most common errors.
